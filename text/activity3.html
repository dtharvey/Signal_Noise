When the signal-to-noise ratio is too small there are two broad strategies we can use to increase its value: hardware solutions and software solutions. Hardware solutions are built into the instrument and are not of interest here. With a software solution, we process the data using a mathematical algorithm as it is collected or after data collection is complete.

Signal Averaging

The simplest software approach is to average a set of \(n\) individual spectra. Because the analyte’s signal is nominally the same for each spectrum, its value increases linearly with \(n\); noise, however, is random and increases as the square root of \(n\); thus \[\left( S_\text{analyte} \right)_n = n \times S_\text{analyte} \quad \quad \quad \left( s_\text{noise} \right)_n = \sqrt{n} \times s_\text{noise}\] Exercise 1. What does a plot of \((S/N)_n\) as a function of \(n\) look like? Suppose a single scan has a signal-to-noise ratio of 1.0. What is the signal-to-noise ratio if \(n = 4\)? What value of \(n\) will yield a signal-to-noise ratio of 4.0?
