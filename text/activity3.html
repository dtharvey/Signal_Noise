When the signal-to-noise ratio is insufficient for our purpose, there are two broad strategies we can use to improve its value: hardware solutions and software solutions. Hardware solutions are built into the instrument and are not of interest here. With a software, or computational solution, we process the data using a mathematical algorithm as it is collected or after data collection is complete.

Signal Averaging

The simplest computational approach is to average a set of \(n\) individual spectra Because the analyte’s signal is nominally the same for each spectrum, the signal increases linearly with \(n\); noise, however, is random and increases as the square root of \(n\); thus \[\left( S_\text{analyte} \right)_n = n \times S_\text{analyte} \quad \quad \quad \left( s_\text{noise} \right)_n = \sqrt{n} \times s_\text{noise}\] Exercise 1. Given these two equations, derive an equation that expresses the signal-to-noise ratio after \(n\) scans, \((S/N)_n\), as a function of the signal-to-noise ratio for one scan, \((S/N)_1\), and the number of scans, \(n\).
